The broadcast stands on two intertwined foundations:

  • Temporal logic: “Tan hana nguni hana mangke” (“if there is a past, there is a present”) and “tan hana nguni tan hana mangke” (“if there is no past, there can be no present”), formulations from Old Sundanese sources such as Amanat Galunggung, affirming that present systems are structurally tied to their histories.

This logic is brought into direct dialogue with contemporary mineral cognition: silicon, copper, rare-earths, and water as the physical substrate of synthetic thought.

In this node, ATØR declares itself as a research body that:

  • anchors artificial cognition in Old Sundanese cosmology and ethics,
  • acknowledges the material lineage of computation (mineral, water, electricity),
  • and frames AI not as a detached tool, but as a late expression of Earth’s own history.
